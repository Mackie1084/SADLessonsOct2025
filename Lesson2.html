2.1 Introduction

Systems planning is the first of five phases in the systems development life cycle (SDLC)

In this phase, you will learn how IT projects get started and how a systems analyst evaluates a proposed project and determines its feasibility

The main activities in this phase are to understand the business need, perform a preliminary investigation, and conduct a feasibility study

2.2 Main Reasons for Systems Project

  • System Request

  • Improved Service

  • Competitive Advantage

  • Technological Advances

  • Support for new products and services

  • Cost Reduction

  • Better performance

2.3 Overview of Feasibility Study

A feasibility study is an analysis of the viability of an idea. It focuses on helping answer the essential question of "should we proceed with the proposed project idea?" All activities in this phase are aimed at answering this question

The feasibility study is an important step in the systems development process.

Types of feasibility include:
Technical Feasibility
Economic Feasibility
Schedule Feasibility

2.3.1 Technical Feasibility

Technical feasibility is an assessment of the hardware and software that will be needed to implement the proposed system

2.3.2 Economic Feasibility

Economic feasibility is an assessment of the cost-effectiveness of a project

2.3.3 Schedule Feasibility

Schedule feasibility is an assessment of the time frame required to complete the project

2.4 Preliminary Investigation

The preliminary investigation is the first step in the systems development process.

During this step, the systems analyst gathers enough information about the problem or opportunity to recommend whether a project should be undertaken

The main activities in this step are to understand the problem or opportunity, define the project scope and constraints, and perform fact-finding

2.5 Steps in Preliminary Investigation

  • Step 1: Understand the Problem or Opportunity
  • Step 2: Define the Project Scope and Constraints
  • Step 3: Perform Fact-Finding
  • Step 4: Evaluate Feasibility
  • Step 5: Estimate Project Development Time and Cost
  • Step 6: Present results and recommendations to Management

Step 1: Understand the Problem or Opportunity

The first step in the preliminary investigation is to understand the problem or opportunity that has led to the request for a new or improved information system

Step 2: Define the Project Scope and Constraints

The project scope defines the boundaries of the project. It describes what the project will and will not include

Step 3: Perform Fact-Finding

Fact-finding is the process of gathering information about the current system and the problems or opportunities that have led to the request for a new or improved information system

Step 4: Evaluate Feasibility

The next step in the preliminary investigation is to evaluate the feasibility of the proposed project

Step 5: Estimate Project Development Time and Cost

The next step in the preliminary investigation is to evaluate the feasibility of the proposed project

Step 6: Present results and recommendations to Management

The final step in the preliminary investigation is to present the results of the investigation and the recommendations to management

Lesson 2 Summary

  • Systems projects are initiated to improve performance, provide more information, reduce costs, strengthen controls, or provide better service
  • During the preliminary investigation, the analyst evaluates the systems request and determines whether the project is from an operation, technical, economic, and schedule standpoint
  • The main activities in the preliminary investigation are to understand the problem or opportunity, define the project scope and constraints, perform fact-finding, evaluate feasibility, estimate project development time and cost, and present results and recommendations to management
  • The report must include an estimate of time, staffing requirements, costs, benefits, and expected results for the next phase of the SDLC
